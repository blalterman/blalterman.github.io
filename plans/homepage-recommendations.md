# Homepage Update Recommendations

**Date:** 2025-11-12
**Purpose:** Strategic recommendations for strengthening homepage connection to Ben narrative
**Context:** Based on integrated thematic and career positioning analyses

---

## Executive Summary

**What Works Well:**
- ✅ Establishes cosmic stakes through accessible, universal question
- ✅ Multi-generational temporal framing (200 generations) perfectly prefigures Ben pages' systems approach
- ✅ Constraint-driven inquiry pattern (can't travel → must study) matches methodology across site
- ✅ Highly concrete example (Proxima Centauri) grounds abstract concepts

**Critical Gaps:**
- ❌ No preview of collaboration theme (central to all Ben pages)
- ❌ Voice transition from universal "we" to personal "I" happens implicitly
- ❌ Systems thinking vocabulary absent (though it's the meta-theme of Ben pages)
- ❌ Bridge text underserves professional evaluation audiences

**Strategic Positioning:**
- Homepage optimized for **inspirational engagement** (public, students)
- Underserves **professional evaluation** (hiring committees, collaborators, funding agencies)

---

## Recommendation Tiers

### Tier 1: Immediate (High Impact, Low Effort)

Quick wins that strengthen narrative coherence without structural changes.

#### 1.1 Strengthen Bridge Text to Ben Pages

**Current:**
```
Want to learn more about my research vision and team philosophy? Read my full story.
```

**Issues:**
- "Team philosophy" undersells breadth (also covers mentorship, open science, AI ethics)
- "My full story" sounds biographical but Ben pages are methodological/philosophical
- Doesn't explain value proposition (why click?)

**Recommended Options:**

**Option A (For Breadth):**
```
Learn about my approach to collaborative science, research leadership, open science,
and how I build teams that tackle questions spanning generations →
```
*Rationale: Previews all five Ben page themes, signals leadership, connects to homepage's temporal framing*

**Option B (For Professional Audiences):**
```
Explore my research vision, team culture, mentorship philosophy, and approach
to building scientific systems that drive discovery →
```
*Rationale: Uses "systems" vocabulary, emphasizes building/leadership, appeals to hiring committees*

**Option C (For Clarity):**
```
Discover how I build collaborative research systems to answer questions that
transcend individual careers →
```
*Rationale: Most concise, directly connects homepage's multi-generational framing to systems approach*

**Recommended choice:** Option C for immediate implementation, with A/B as alternatives based on audience priority.

#### 1.3 Clarify Habitability Framing

**Current Issue:** Tension between "Are we alone?" (astrobiology/exoplanet focus) and Research Vision's habitability/Earth-Sun framing.

**Current:**
```
So how can we know if there is other life in the universe? By studying how a star
sustains life on the one planet we are certain it exists.
```

**Recommended Revision:**
```
So how can we know if there is other life in the universe? By understanding how stars
create habitable zones—starting with the one star-planet system where we know life
exists: our Sun and Earth.
```

**Rationale:**
- "Habitable zones" connects astrobiology to solar physics
- Explicitly names Sun and Earth (removes ambiguity)
- Bridges homepage question to Research Vision's focus

### Tier 2: Medium-Term (Moderate Effort, High Strategic Value)

Additions that require new content but significantly strengthen narrative.

#### 2.1 Add Personal Voice Paragraph

**Challenge:** Homepage uses third-person "we" (humanity), Ben pages use first-person "I". Transition happens implicitly.

**Recommendation:** Add brief third paragraph that bridges voice and previews approach.

**Proposed New Paragraph (after current content):**
```
I study the solar wind—the stream of charged particles our Sun continuously emits—
to understand how it shapes habitability over timescales from seconds to centuries.
Through collaborative research that crosses institutions and generations, I build
the scientific infrastructure to answer humanity's oldest question.
```

**What this adds:**
- ✅ Introduces first-person "I" voice
- ✅ Names specific research focus (solar wind)
- ✅ Bridges timescales (seconds to centuries, connects to 200 generations)
- ✅ Seeds collaboration and infrastructure/systems language
- ✅ Connects personal work back to cosmic question

**Alternative (more leadership-focused):**
```
I lead collaborative research into the solar wind—the stream of charged particles
our Sun continuously emits—building teams and infrastructure that study how stars
sustain life across timescales from seconds to centuries. Through systematic
approaches that transcend individual careers, we're creating the foundations to
answer humanity's oldest question.
```

**What the alternative adds:**
- Stronger leadership language ("lead," "building teams")
- Systems vocabulary ("infrastructure," "systematic approaches")
- Better positioning for hiring committees

**Recommended choice:** Use alternative version if career positioning is priority; use first version if maintaining inspirational tone is priority.

### Tier 3: Long-Term (Structural Considerations)

Strategic options requiring larger architectural decisions.

#### 3.1 Dual Landing Experience Strategy

**Concept:** Recognize that homepage serves different audiences with conflicting needs.

**Current state:** Single homepage optimized for inspiration/accessibility

**Proposed:**
- **Homepage (/):** Optimized for public engagement, student recruitment (keep inspirational tone)
- **Research/About section:** Optimized for professional evaluation (add leadership positioning content)

**Implementation approaches:**

**Approach A: Keep homepage as-is, strengthen /ben overview**
- Homepage remains universal entry point
- /ben overview page explicitly addresses different audiences
- Add intro paragraph: "Whether you're a potential collaborator, student, hiring committee member, or simply curious about how modern science works, these pages explore my approach to research, collaboration, and scientific leadership."

**Approach B: Add "Professional Overview" page**
- Create new top-level page (/about or /leadership)
- Consolidates professional positioning content
- Linked prominently from homepage for professional audiences
- Allows homepage to maintain inspirational focus

**Approach C: Conditional content on homepage**
- Use design to serve multiple audiences on single page
- Section 1: Inspirational (current content)
- Section 2: Professional snapshot (leadership, track record, capabilities)
- Both sections visible but visually distinct

**Recommended choice:** Approach A (strengthen /ben overview) is lowest effort and maintains current architecture.

## Strategic Insights

### 1. Tension Between Accessibility and Positioning

Homepage currently optimizes for **universal accessibility** (good for public engagement, student recruitment) at the expense of **professional positioning** (needed for hiring committees, collaborators, leadership roles).

**Two paths forward:**
- **Path A:** Accept this tradeoff, use homepage for inspiration and rely on Ben pages for professional evaluation
- **Path B:** Add subtle professional signals (leadership language, systems vocabulary) without sacrificing accessibility

**Recommended:** Path B (Version 2 above) - the personal voice paragraph bridges inspiration and professionalism.

### 2. Voice Transition is Key Opportunity

The implicit transition from "we" (humanity) to "I" (personal approach) is the single biggest gap. Adding one paragraph that makes this transition explicit would significantly strengthen narrative coherence.

### 3. Systems Vocabulary Seeds Expectations

Homepage currently doesn't prepare readers for the systems-heavy language in Ben pages. Adding even a few strategic terms ("collaborative systems," "infrastructure," "systematic approaches") would smooth this transition.

### 4. Temporal Framing is Your Strength

The "200 generations" framing is incredibly powerful—it's the most dramatic temporal scale across your entire site. It perfectly justifies the systems approach. Don't lose this strength when making changes.
